### Huberman Lab: "Essentials: Using Light to Optimize Health" [1]

**Episode Summary**:  
Andrew Huberman synthesizes research on how different wavelengths of light (sunlight, UVB, red/infrared) impact human physiology, offering practical protocols for optimizing sleep, mood, hormone regulation, and skin health.

**Highlights**:
- Morning sunlight exposure regulates melatonin, improves sleep, and supports hormone cycles.
- UVB light exposure linked to increased pain tolerance, immune function, and seasonal mood regulation.
- Red/infrared light therapy shown to aid wound healing, skin health, and offset age-related vision decline.
- Cautions against excessive melatonin supplementation and nighttime light exposure.
- Provides specific timing/frequency protocols for light exposure.

**Insights**:
- Light is a potent environmental lever for health, with protocols increasingly evidence-based.
- Overuse of supplements (e.g., melatonin) may disrupt natural hormonal rhythms.
